Growth, Functional and Bioinformatic Analyses of Photosynthetic Strategies of Prochlorococcus Strains Under Varying Oxygen

Julie Nadeau1, Mireille Savoie1, Douglas Campbell1


1 Department of Biochemistry, Mount Allison University

Introduction

Photosynthesis is the fundamental process in the biosphere. Prochlorococcus:

  • Photosynthetic unicellular cyanobacterium
  • Important in global carbon cycling, Prochlorococcus contributes significantly to primary production in the open oceans.
  • Strains occupy a wide range of habitats.
  • With climate change, ocean warming causing a decrease of oxygen solubility at the surface, Prochlorococcus is expected to benefit, increasing its distribution and global abundance.

    Figure 1: The biological interactions of Prochlorococcus with some key players in the marine carbon cycle. Adapted from (Cai et al., 2023).

    We use:

  • Bioptical measures of growth and photosynthesis
  • Bioinformatics analysis of strain capacities

Objectives

  1. Determine whether Prochlorococcus strains are constitutively able to accomodate changes in oxygen, or whether they acclimate over a period of time to different levels of oxygen.
  2. To provide insights into the potential ecological niches of Prochlorococcus strains.

Methods

Bioptical Analysis of Growth Rates

Three strains of Prochlorococcus, MED4, MIT9313, SS120

Multi-Cultivator monitors OD680 (Chlorophyll and scattering) and OD720 (cell scattering)

Applies combinations:

  • Dissolved O2
    • 230-280 µM (full O2)
    • 25-50 µM (intermediate O2)
    • 0.5-5.0 µM (low O2)

  • Photoperiod

    • 12 h

  • Light level

    • 30 µmol photons m-2 s-1
    • 90 µmol photons m-2 s-1
    • 180 µmol photons m-2 s-1

  • Waveband

    • 450 ∓ 45 nm (Blue)

all at 22°C, to each of 8 separate tubes.

Centrifuged samples and the cell pellets frozen in liquid nitrogen and preserved in an -80 oC freezer. Enables future transcriptomic analyses.

Bioptical Functional Measurements

  • Expose samples to a series of increasing light levels and track a ‘light response’ curve of photosynthetic function of Photosystem II, using Solisense FRRf Instrument.
  • Expose samples to a series of increasing light levels and track a ‘light response’ curve of photosynthetic function of Photosystem I and Photosystem II in parallel, using Dual-PAM-100 Instrument. The oxygen levels tested included:
    • 230-280 µM (full O2)
    • 25-50 µM (intermediate O2)
    • 0.5-5.0 µM (low O2)

  • Whole Cell Absorbance Spectra using Olis Spectrophotometer. The oxygen levels tested included:
    • 230-280 µM (full O2)
    • 0.5-5.0 µM (low O2)

Chlorophyll a concentrations determined using the Turner Trilogy or Spectrophotometer on each sample

Figure 5: Analysis of Maximum PSII Electron Transport Rate (Pmax) for Prochlorococcus MED4 (Clade HLI) and MIT9313 (Clade LLIV). Pmax, derived from light response curve fits (FIGURE)), is plotted vs. measurement oxygen concentrations, with error bars representing the standard error of the estimate. Data is grouped by strain and growth light level, colours indicate different growth oxygen levels.

Across both strains, a trend is observed, indicating that Pmax values exhibit variation corresponding to changes in oxygen concentration. Specifically, strain MED4 demonstrates relatively consistent Pmax values across different oxygen concentrations, although with minor fluctuations. Since Pmax remains reletively stable across varying oxygen concentrations, this suggests that the cells are constitutively adapted to cope with changes in oxygen levels.

However, strain MIT9313 exhibits more pronounced differences in Pmax values, particularly evident between the lowest (2 µM) and highest (250 µM) oxygen levels. These findings underscore the strain-specific responses of Prochlorococcus to varying oxygen concentrations, suggesting a significant influence of oxygen levels on the PSII electron transport capacity of these strains. Next Steps

In addition to the experiments conducted so far, transcriptomic analyses could provide insights into the molecular mechanisms underlying different Prochlorococcus strains responses to varying oxygen concentrations. By examining changes in gene expression patterns, particularly those associated with oxygen metabolism and photosynthesis regulation, researchers can identify key genes and pathways involved in the cellular response to different oxygen levels. Transcriptomic analyses would allow for a comprehensive understanding of how different Prochlorococcus strains modulates there gene expression profile to adapt to fluctuating environmental conditions, providing a deeper understanding into the cellular strategies employed by different Prochlorococcus strains to thrive in diverse marine ecosystems.
